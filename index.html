Confidence Intervals A confidence interval is two points (the lower and upper limits) between which the unknown population quantity being estimated lies with a certain probability. Heuristic Formula 𝑷𝒐𝒊𝒏𝒕 𝑬𝒔𝒕𝒊𝒎𝒂𝒕𝒆 ± (𝑫𝒊𝒔𝒕𝒓𝒊𝒃𝒖𝒕𝒊𝒐𝒏 𝑷𝒆𝒓𝒄𝒆𝒏𝒕𝒊𝒍𝒆) × (𝑺𝒕𝒂𝒏𝒅𝒂𝒓𝒅 𝑬𝒓𝒓𝒐𝒓)

  • Each of the quantities – the point estimate, the percentile, and the standard error – depends on what is being estimated. Consult the formulas on the following pages to see how.
  • The percentile additionally depends on the level of confidence, (e.g.) 90%, 95%, 99%, etc.
  • Remember that this heuristic formula does not apply for confidence intervals for variances and ratios of variances.
